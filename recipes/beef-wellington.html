Beef Wellington

Juicy, tender and succulent!

Only 5 parts to make this dish that will leave you wanting for more! Lets start from the inside and work our way towards the outside.

Ingredients

  • Beef tenderloin
  • Duxelle (finely chopped mixed mushrooms)
  • Prosciutto di Parma
  • Crepe 16" x 12"
  • 2x puff pastry 16" x 12"

Steps

  1. Prepare a 20 cm cut of beef tenderloin, season it well with salt and wrap with plastic. Rest for 2 hours to allow the salt to penetrate the meat. Afterwards, sear on all sides over high heat and allow to rest.
  2. Choose mushrooms of your liking and chop finely. Cook in a pan to remove the moisture and put aside.
  3. Start with a crepe that is 16" x 12" and cover with thin slices of Prosciutto di Parma.
  4. Evenly spread a thin layer of duxelle all over the crepe.
  5. Place the beef tenderloin on top of the duxelle and tightly wrap it in the crepe.
  6. Wrap the whole crepe in plastic wrap and further wrap by twisting the ends of the plastic wrap (the tigher the better!). Rest in refrigerator for 1 hour.
  7. Lay out the puff pastry and brush the inside with a thin layer of egg wash. Wrap the crepe well and trim with a knife leaving enough pastry on the side to seal with a fork.
  8. Fold the sides of the puff pastry under and apply egg wash all over the outside.
  9. (Optional)Lattic shape decoration with the under puff pastry. Trim the decoration to size and apply egg wash.
  10. Season the top with course ground sea salt which will help to crust up the puff pastry even more.
  11. Bake the beef wellington on a baking tray at 400 Fahrenheit for 20-25 minutes for a medium rare cook. Internal temperature should be around 118 Fahrenheit. Always remember to allow time for rest before slicing.
